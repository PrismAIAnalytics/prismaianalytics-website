# LinkedIn pinned post — FDE-model mid-market

## Body (paste verbatim)

The "forward deployed engineer" role is having a moment — 800% job posting growth, Salesforce committing to 1,000 FDEs, Deloitte standing up a named Anthropic FDE practice.

Here's what's underdiscussed: the FDE model breaks at the mid-market.

A $280K embedded engineer is the right answer for a Fortune 100 with a $5M deployment budget. It's the wrong economics for a $200M PE-backed manufacturer who needs Claude operationalized across three workflows by Q3.

That gap is what Prism AI Analytics was built for. Same delivery pattern — engineers inside the customer environment, building production agents against real data, not slideware. Different scope, different cost structure, different speed-to-value.

And in regulated industries specifically — financial services, anything with KRI/OPI frameworks and audit posture — the FDE model needs someone who already speaks compliance fluently. That's where my background lives: enterprise data engineering at one of the four largest U.S. banks before founding Prism.

If you're a systems integrator ramping FDE capacity and need regulated mid-market depth on a subcontracted basis, let's talk.
