# LinkedIn personal post — CM-AI Part 2 founder reflection (Thu 5/28)

## Body (paste verbatim)

I keep watching AI governance teams ship more observability and call it governance.

It isn't.

Observability documents what the agent did. Governance defines what good looks like, detects deviation in real time, and intervenes at machine speed. The first is a receipt. The second is a control.

That gap — between machine-speed action and human-speed oversight — has a name. It's the latency gap. And the disciplines that close it already exist.

I spent ~7.5 years inside one of the four largest U.S. banks before founding Prism. Configuration management and Key Risk Indicator frameworks have been the operational backbone of regulated industry for thirty years. They're not new. They've just never been formally ported to AI agents.

That's the work Prism is doing.

Today we published Part 2 of the CM-AI series — Configuration Management for AI Agents: The Missing Twin of Agentic Governance. Four disciplines. Three delivery layers. A KRI tier model that lets governance run at the speed of the agent, not the speed of human attention.

The piece on the company page is the institutional version. This one is mine — the founder seat, the reason I'm building this.

If you're inside a regulated organization deploying agents and trying to operationalize governance beyond a dashboard, this is the framework I'd hand you on day one.
