## Voice litmus self-check

Cowork's own pre-pass on the draft above. Surfaces the spots that drifted closest to founder-warmth, generic-consultancy, or marketing-hyperbole registers. Brand pod (BS-004) reviewer can decide whether each is acceptable or needs a rewrite.

- **"Without the enterprise overhead" (About headline)** — edges toward founder-warmth positioning. Implies a value-for-money claim that could read as "small shop, big results" framing. Alternative: "AI adoption strategy and execution, scoped to operating reality."
- **"The first conversation is free" (Contact intro)** — borderline. Softens the institutional voice toward sales friendliness. If Michele's intent is to be inviting rather than purely authoritative, it stays. Otherwise: "Engagements begin with a scoped diagnostic or a direct conversation."
- **"Not just at launch" (Operate badge)** — "just" is a mildly informal hedge. Rewrite candidate: "Run production AI and compliance operations continuously across the full governance lifecycle."
- **Accolade 2, "not just display data"** — same pattern. "Not just" appears twice in close proximity in the About section. Both work individually; together they read as a rhetorical tic. One should be cut or restructured.
- **Service card descriptions under Architect** — strongest-performing block for specificity (Ansible, PowerShell, Terraform, DAX named). The Operate cards are slightly weaker — "executive and board-level reporting" in the Stakeholder Reporting card is institutional but generic. A named framework or deliverable format would sharpen it.
- **"An order of magnitude" (Claude card 3)** — strong claim made without a cited example. Not a statistic, so does not violate the no-hallucinated-stats rule, but may read as marketing hyperbole to a technically literate buyer. Consider softening to "significantly reduces" or backing it with a framing like "what was a six-week manual process in traditional audit prep."
- **Sizing footer line under the journey band** — running long at ~52 words. Stream B should consider whether this becomes two lines visually, or whether the productized offering names move to a bulleted sub-element so the sentence can shorten.

## Brand application notes (Cowork → BS-004 reviewer)

- **Voice:** Institutional third-person throughout. No "I help," no "we help," no founder-personal warmth register. Michele is named once in the About body in third person to establish practitioner credibility without centering personal narrative.
- **Tone:** Formal · moderate energy · high technical depth in compliance cards, moderate technical depth in analytics cards, low technical depth in hero and journey band. Energy stays flat and authoritative — no acceleration language ("supercharge," "unlock," "transform").
- **Messages:** All three positioning pillars present — strategy+execution unity (hero, subhead, About lead), sizing-to-need (journey band sizing footer, subhead, About lead), and compliance-engineering depth (Assess service cards, Claude cards, trust bar Govern group).
- **Terminology:** CIS, DISA STIGs, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act, CMMC 2.0, Ansible, Terraform, PowerShell, ISO/IEC 42001 named explicitly. "Boutique," "tailored solutions," "complexity to clarity," "trusted partner," "remarkable" all excised. "Engagement" and "Productized" used as structural pill labels rather than prose terms.
- **Adaptations:** "Assess / Architect / Operate" held as the top-level arc across all sections. "Discover / Automate / Govern" from the compliance positioning doc is reserved for compliance-specific internal framing and does not appear at page level. Pricing pulled verbatim from `Marketing/Compliance_Services_Positioning.md` for productized cards that have documented prices; cards without documented prices (AI Starter Kit, Lunch & Learn, Data Health Audit) carry no price anchor to avoid unsupported claims.
